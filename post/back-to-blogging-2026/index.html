Back to Blogging: AI is Moving Fast

It has been a while. Last time I posted here I was still finishing up at deCODE. Since then I moved back to Iceland, changed jobs, changed jobs again, and the blog just sort of stopped. Not a conscious decision — more like one of those things where each week you think “I should write something” and then you don’t, and eventually enough weeks pass that the whole thing feels like it needs a restart.

So here’s the restart.

Why now?

Honestly, AI development is moving so fast that I feel like I have something to say. I work with data and statistics for a living, so my angle is specific: what does this actually mean for quantitative work? What changes, what doesn’t, and what are the parts nobody is really talking about?

I find myself having these conversations at work all the time. Where these tools actually add value. Where they don’t. What the gap is between the hype and what happens when you try to use them for real things. Might as well write it down.

What to expect

I’m not turning this into a news feed. I’d rather write less often but actually say something — work through an idea, share something I built, or write up something that took me way longer to figure out than it should have.

Topics I’m thinking about:

  • LLM internals — how these models actually work, from attention to RLHF to inference-time scaling
  • AI in science — what tools like AlphaFold and the wave of AI-assisted research mean for fields like genetics
  • Practical tooling — what’s actually useful day-to-day versus what’s a demo
  • Statistics meets ML — the old tensions and the new syntheses

Getting started again

The blog got a makeover too — migrated from Hugo to Astro, cleaner design, dark mode that actually works. Small things, but they make it more pleasant to write.

If you want to follow along, there’s a subscribe link in the nav. Or find me on Twitter/X at @gumgumeo.

Let’s see where this goes.
